Boeing 747

Operations

The next major order of Boeing aircraft for BOAC was for 11 747-100s the worlds first widebodied aircraft. On 22 April 1970 BOAC received its first 747, but the aircraft did not enter commercial service until 14 April 1971 due to BOAC's inability to settle crewing and pay rates with the British Air Line Pilots' Association. BOAC's successor British Airways later became the largest Boeing customer outside North America. The 747 or Jumbo Jet would be used on many routes to the middle east and far east destinations like the famous Hong Kong Kai Tak airport. However, the aircraft became most famous for being the powerhouse of cross Atlantic flights used by almost all airlines that operated these routes like BOAC, PanAm, Delta and many others.

Cockpit 

Cockpit technology hadn't move to much on with VOR navigation still being used and stil fitted with a Boeing style cockpit similar to the 707. The big leap was with the engines being more powerfull then ever, as well as being the first widebodied aircraft for passenger use.    

Interior 

The introduction of the Jumbo Jet allowed airlines to fit more passengers on-board then ever before causing the fall in the cost of flight and allowed more and more people to fly and travel well into the 70s and 80s. The 747 proved so successful with BOAC and then BA so much that till this day newer variance of the aircraft still fly.
