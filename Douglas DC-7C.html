Douglas DC-7C

 

 

Operations

BOAC needed a new aircraft for the Atlantic crossings and during the mid 1950s BOAC saw how reliable and quick the DC7 was through Pa Aam's operations with the aircraft. With the delays to the development and production of the Bristol Britannia BOAC was forced to respod to PanAm by ording the C vairent of the aircraf to use on cross Atlantic routes. The DC-7 sold much better then the updated constalation, the starliner as the aircraft design was getting old with the rapid movement of aircraft development at the time. However sales were cut short by the arrival of the Jet age in the late 50s with the arrival of the Comet but mostly because of the B707 that sold in there thousand.         

General characteristics

Crew: 5 flight crew, 4 flight attendants
Capacity: up to 105 Passengers + 18,440 lb (8,360 kg) cargo/baggage
Length: 112 ft 3 in (34.21 m)
Wingspan: 127 ft 6 in (38.86 m)
Height: 31 ft 8 in (9.65 m)
Wing area: 1,637 sq ft (152.1 m2)
Empty weight: 72,763 lb (33,005 kg)
Operating weight empty: 78,890 lb (35,780 kg)
Max takeoff weight: 143,000 lb (64,864 kg)
Maximum landing weight: 109,000 lb (49,000 kg)
Fuel capacity: 7,824 US gal (6,515 imp gal; 29,620 l) in eight wing tanks
Powerplant: 4 × Wright R-3350-988TC18EA1-2 18-cylinder turbo-compound air-cooled radial piston engines, 3,400 hp (2,500 kW)
Propellers: 4-bladed Hamilton-Standard Hydromatic 34E60-345, 14 ft 0 in (4.27 m) diameter constant-speed fully-feathering reversible propellers
Performance
 
Maximum speed: 406 mph (653 km/h, 353 kn) , rated power in high blower at 22,700 ft (6,900 m)
Cruise speed: 346 mph (557 km/h, 301 kn) recommended at 21,600 ft (6,600 m) and 110,000 lb (50,000 kg) A.U.W.
Stall speed: 97 mph (156 km/h, 84 kn) at landing weight
Range: 5,635 mi (9,069 km, 4,897 nmi) , max fuel, 15,310 lb (6,940 kg) payload at 274 mph (238 kn; 441 km/h) at 15,000 ft (4,600 m) with no allowances 4,635 mi (4,028 nmi; 7,459 km), max payload with no allowances
Service ceiling: 21,700 ft (6,600 m) at max A.U.W. 14,600 ft (4,500 m) on three engines at max A.U.W.
Rate of climb: 240 ft/min (1.2 m/s) at 20,000 ft (6,100 m) at max A.U.W.
Take-off field length: 6,360 ft (1,940 m) at max A.U.W. Landing run from 50 ft (15 m): 5,100 ft (1,600 m)
